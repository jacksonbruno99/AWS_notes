Day 1 – AWS Global Infrastructure

Regions

  • A Region is a geographical area containing two or more Availability Zones (AZs).
  • Each Region is isolated — no automatic replication unless configured.
  • Region choice impacts latency, cost, data residency, and service availability.
  • Not all services are available in every Region — check AWS Regional Services List.

Availability Zones (AZs)

  • AZ = one or more physically separate data centers with independent power, cooling, networking.
  • AZs connected via low-latency links (<1–2 ms).
  • Best practice: deploy EC2, RDS, load balancers across ≥2 AZs for high availability.

Edge Locations

  • Used by CloudFront, Route 53, Global Accelerator.
  • Global cache nodes bring content closer to users.
  • Edge ≠ AZ — Edge = content delivery, not compute.

Local & Wavelength Zones

  • Local Zones reduce latency in metro areas (media, gaming, etc.).
  • Wavelength Zones = 5G ultra-low-latency apps (telecom edge).

Fault Tolerance Concepts

  • Multi-AZ deployments survive single AZ failures.
  • Multi-Region = disaster recovery and global resilience.
  • S3 & DynamoDB = multi-AZ by default; EC2, RDS, EBS = single-AZ unless configured.
